About me

"I want inside this night that is longer than life. I want, inside this night, life raw and bloody and full of saliva. I want this word: splendidness, splendidness is the fruit of succulence, fruit without sadness. I want distances. My wild intuition about myself. But my main thing is always hidden. I am implicit. And when I make myself explicit I lose the humid intimacy." -- Clarice Lispector, Agua Viva

I've been working in computational neuroscience the past 3 years of my PhD under the guise of a program of information engineering at the University of Pisa. I enjoy learning about neural mechanisms, representations of cognitive states in the brain, emergent phenomenon that come from dynamic and complex systems, and physics in general.

When I'm not doing that, you can find me writing poetry, playing my guitar or learning a new instrument, learning and creating audiovisual arts, and reading books.
